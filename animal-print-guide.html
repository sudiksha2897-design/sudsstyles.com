How to wear

the right way

It's not as scary as it looks. Once you know the rules, it's actually one of the easiest prints to wear.

The Big Rule
Anchor the colour 

Every animal print has a main background colour hiding inside it. Find it. Then wear that same colour somewhere else in your outfit — a bag, shoes, a belt. That one move makes the whole look planned.

Before You Leave the House
Quick checklist ✅
  • One animal print piece only
    Just one. Let it do the talking. Everything else should be a plain solid colour.
  • Anchor colour matched
    You've pulled the background colour of the print into at least one other item — shoe, bag, or belt.
  • Print size fits your frame
    Big bold print for bigger frames. Small tight print for petite frames.
  • No other patterns in the outfit
    Stripes, florals, checks — put them away. Animal print doesn't share the spotlight.
  • Take a picture before you go
    If the print is the first thing you notice — great. If it feels like too much — change just one item.
Tips and Tricks
Simple things that make it work €,;
01
It's a neutral, not a statement
Think of it like denim or stripes. It goes with lots of things once you stop being scared of it.
02
Start with a small piece
Try a leopard belt or snake print bag first. Way easier than a full outfit.
03
Zebra goes with almost anything
Black and white works with every colour. Easiest animal print to start with.
04
Fabric matters a lot
The same print in silk looks luxe. In cheap fabric, it looks costume. Quality makes the difference.
05
Match your tones
Leopard top + camel trousers + tan boots = instant elevated outfit. All in the same colour family.
06
When in doubt, go smaller
A smaller print is always safer. Looks like texture from far away and pattern up close.
What Goes Wrong
4 mistakes to avoid ❌
01
Mixing it with another pattern
Florals + leopard = chaos. The eye doesn't know where to look.
Fix
Everything else should be a plain solid colour.
02
Wrong print size for your body
A huge print on a small person is too much. A tiny print on a tall person disappears.
Fix
Match the boldness of the print to the boldness of your frame.
03
Random accessories that don't connect
Leopard dress + red bag + silver shoes + white jacket. Each fine alone — together, confusing.
Fix
Pull the anchor colour into one accessory. That connection ties it all together.
04
Wearing two animal prints at once
Unless it's the exact same print, two animal prints together usually looks accidental.
Fix
One print piece per outfit. Simple rule, big difference.
